Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Senior Care

We know that finding the right support for a loved one brings many questions. Here are answers to the topics families ask us most often when considering Golf View Home for assisted living and 24 hour care for seniors.

Who is assisted living right for?

Assisted living is ideal for seniors who value their independence but need daily support with personal care, medication management, or mobility. If you or a loved one find that managing a household alone has become challenging, our residential setting provides the perfect balance of autonomy and care.

What does daily support include?

Daily support at Golf View Home is personalized to each resident. It typically includes assistance with bathing, dressing, and grooming, as well as medication reminders, nutritious home-cooked meals, housekeeping, and coordination with healthcare providers. Our goal is to handle the details so residents can enjoy their day.

How do we discuss care needs with our loved one?

Approach the conversation with empathy and focus on enhancing quality of life rather than a loss of independence. Highlight the benefits of companionship, safety, and freedom from household chores. Our care team is also happy to help families navigate this sensitive discussion with warmth and understanding.

What is the atmosphere like at Golf View Home?

Our Brentwood residence is designed to feel like a family home, not an institution. With sunlit common areas, a lush garden view, and a calm, caring rhythm to each day, residents enjoy a warm and familiar environment. We foster a true sense of belonging, where staff and residents become like extended family.

Can families stay involved in care?

Absolutely. We believe family involvement is essential to a resident's wellbeing. Loved ones are encouraged to visit, participate in activities, and join us for meals. We maintain open communication with families about health updates, daily life, and any changes in care needs, ensuring you are always part of the journey.

How do we start the conversation about moving?

Starting the conversation is often the hardest step. We suggest beginning with a casual visit to our home for a cup of coffee and a tour. Seeing the warm, social environment firsthand often eases anxiety and opens the door to a positive discussion about the benefits of senior housing independent living in a safe, supportive setting.
